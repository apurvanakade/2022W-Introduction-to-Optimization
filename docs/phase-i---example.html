Introduction to Optimization

Day 5 Phase I - Example

Consider the following linear program:

\[\begin{align} \begin{array}{rrrrrrrr} \mbox{maximize: } & -2x_1 & + & -1x_2 \\ \mbox{subject to: } & -1x_1 & + & 1x_2 & \le & -1 \\ & -1x_1 & + & 2x_2 & \le & -2 \\ & 0x_1 & + & 1x_2 & \le & 1 \\ \end{array} \\ x_1, \: x_2 \ge 0\end{align}\]

The initial dictionary formed using this linear program is not feasible because \(b_1, b_2 < 0\). So, we initiate Phase I to find a feasible dictionary first. The first step is to create an auxiliary linear program.

\[\begin{align} \begin{array}{rrrrrrrrrr} \mbox{maximize: } & 0x_1 & + & 0x_2 & + & -1x_0 \\ \mbox{subject to: } & -1x_1 & + & 1x_2 & + & -1x_0 & \le & -1 \\ & -1x_1 & + & 2x_2 & + & -1x_0 & \le & -2 \\ & 0x_1 & + & 1x_2 & + & -1x_0 & \le & 1 \\ \end{array} \\ x_1, \: x_2, \: x_0 \ge 0\end{align}\]
